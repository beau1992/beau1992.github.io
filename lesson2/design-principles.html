Design Principles

Visual Hierarchy

KSL

Visual hierarchy refers to the arrangement or presentation of elements in a way that implies importance. In other words, visual hierarchy influences the order in which the human eye perceives what it sees. This order is created by the visual contrast between forms in a field of perception.

KSL's website uses the concept of hierarchy by putting the things they feel are most important at the top and making the most important things the biggest and then smaller as they want to demonstrate less attention as you scroll down the page.

Alignment

Wikipedia

Alignment refers to placing text and other design elements on a page so they line up. It helps to create order, organize your elements, create visual connections, and improve the readability of your design.

Wikipedia is pretty much all about information on almost anything. You don't even have to be able to see the content to be able to see that Wikipedia is very good at alignment. Everything lines up and has its place in line to help keep everything organized.

White Space

tripadvisor

In page layout, illustration and sculpture, white space is often referred to as negative space. It is the portion of a page left unmarked: margins, gutters, and space between columns, lines of type, graphics, figures, or objects drawn or depicted.

tripadvisor makes good use of whitespace by having a white background and colorful images and icons to navigate the website.
